My name is Keon Khosravi, I am currently a first-year Neuroscience major at the University of California Riverside. I am currently involved in numerous organizations on campus, such as the American Future- Doctors Association, the STEM club, and also the SHARP club, a club dedicated to helping feed the homeless in the Riverside area. My passion in life is to help others, it is a personal hobby of mine to help those in need, and that is the main reason why I would want to become a doctor. Other hobbies of mine include longboarding, going to the gym, and hanging out with my friends. Many would consider to me be a very extroverted and social individual. I love to converse with everyone, doesn’t matter the subject I can just talk to someone for hours. Other than my personality traits, I believe my work ethic is what sets me apart from others, I will always put my 100% effort into anything I do, even if I am not the greatest at it I will still continue to put my best effort into it no matter what.

I am currently an employee at the on-campus dining hall, Lothian, my job varies from washing dishes to serving food. Although this is not my ideal job, I work with pride, knowing that in the end, it will all be worth it for my end goal. But, this job also teaches me the importance of persistence, working in the dining hall may not be the most appealing job but the lessons you learn while working is what makes it so valuable. Before working at the dining hall, I was an employee at Smart and Final, I was mainly a cashier, but my other responsibilities include, restocking, unloading the daily shipments, and organizing the warehouse freezer. I also had the job as a Sou-Chef at a Hot-Chicken restaurant, and that job really taught me the importance of teamwork, and if one person in the team doesn’t hold his end up the whole team will fail. In my opinion, each job I have worked at taught me all very valuable lessons that attribute to the type of person I am today.

My dream job is to become a doctor, I am not sure what type of doctor I would like to be as of right now, because my main goal is to get into medical school. Whatever experiences I am gaining right now whether that be with work or school, it will, in the end, help me accomplish my end goal. It will also attribute to my overall development as an individual, allowing myself to improve in any way possible. I believe that I will become a doctor one day, and that day will only come with perseverance and dedication to everything I do.
